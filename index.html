Grammar Overview


Reading Passage Comprehension is the ability to understand, interpret, and analyze written text.


Types of questions asked in “Reading Passage Comprehension”:

  • Factual questions: Answering with information directly from the text.
  • Inferential questions: Drawing conclusions based on the information provided.
  • Evaluative questions: Making judgments based on the information presented.
  • Predictive questions: Making informed guesses about what might happen next.


A table to understand steps and strategy for “Reading Passage Comprehension”:
Steps Strategy Description
1. Read the passage carefully Take your time to read the passage thoroughly, paying attention to details.
2. Identify the main idea Determine the central theme or main point the author is trying to convey.
3. Understand keywords Look for important words or phrases and make sure you understand their meaning.
4. Find supporting details Locate facts, examples, or explanations that support the main idea.
5. Make connections Relate the information in the passage to your own knowledge or experiences.
6. Ask questions As you read, ask yourself questions about the content to stay engaged and focused.
7. Summarize the passage After reading, briefly summarize the main points of the passage in your own words.
8. Reflect on the meaning Think about what you learned from the passage and how it relates to the overall topic.
